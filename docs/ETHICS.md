# Ethics

## Why This Document Exists

This product sits close to something we take seriously: how students learn. That's not a neutral place to build a product, and we don't treat it like one. This document lays out the principles we hold ourselves to — not as a legal formality, but as the actual standard the product is designed against.

## Our Core Belief

Cognitive skill doesn't grow from repeating tasks or receiving finished answers — it grows from actively processing information until it makes sense. AI's role here isn't to replace that process. It's to remove the friction around it: the wasted time, the unclear sources, the not knowing where to start. We build so that a student's effort goes toward understanding, not toward fighting the process to get there.

## What We Will Not Build

We will not build, and do not consider it a success if the product enables, any feature whose primary purpose is to help a student misrepresent AI assistance as their own unaided work, evade an academic integrity policy, or produce a final submission with no student involvement in the thinking behind it. If a feature makes cheating easier instead of learning easier, it does not belong in this product, regardless of how impressive or convenient it would be.

## What We Commit To Instead

**Traceability.** Every fact or claim the product surfaces is tied to a real source. Nothing is presented as true just because it sounds right.

**Framing as a starting point, not a finished product.** Drafts, summaries, and generated content are built to be worked with and revised by the student — not submitted as-is.

**Visibility over automation.** Where the product checks or filters its own output — verifying a claim, flagging a weak argument — that process is shown to the student, not hidden. The goal is for students to learn to do this kind of evaluation themselves over time.

**Meeting students honestly.** The product doesn't pretend to be a teacher, a grader, or a substitute for real feedback from a real person. It's a guide, and it says so.

## An Honest Acknowledgment

We know a tool like this can be misused despite these commitments, and we don't think pretending otherwise is honest. Boundaries built into the product reduce misuse; they don't eliminate the possibility of it. We'd rather be upfront about that limitation than overstate what any set of guardrails can guarantee.

## How This Gets Enforced

These principles aren't just stated — they shape actual decisions about what ships. Any new skill or feature is evaluated against this document before release. If something is technically possible but conflicts with these commitments, it doesn't ship. See [`RESPONSIBLE_USE.md`](./RESPONSIBLE_USE.md) for guidance on using the product in a way consistent with these principles.
